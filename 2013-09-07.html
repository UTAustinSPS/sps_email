SPS Activities

  1. Sunday Night Study Session

    Sundays, 6pm-???, RLM 7.116

    It's back! Our Sunday Night Study Session (SNSS) starts up September 8! For those who haven't been, every week we invite any and all physics majors and people in physics or math classes to come by to work on homework. With many upper-division students in attendance (and some LAs for classes), when you run into problems, you won't be stuck for long. We also provide cookies, coffee, tea, etc., to help keep you caffeinated energized. Some come by to help or be helped! If you're interested in tutoring, making known that you're available during the night (name on a chalkboard) is a great way to earn service hours for Sigma Pi Sigma.

  2. Sigma Pi Sigma Service Hours

    Interested in joining Sigma Pi Sigma, the physics honors society? The requirements are listed on our website. For the service hour component, we will be managing that through the new HornsLink system (see below for the link). Join the SPS group and when you volunteer (like tutoring for SNSS above), submit a request for us to verify your service hours. That way, we have a nice record of volunteers and volunteer time (and it will show up on the resume HornsLink can create for you)! You input the time you worked, we sign off, then you don't have to think about it again!

Careers

  1. Career Design Center

    The College of Natural Sciences' Career Design Center is hosting several events this semester for career prep and other interesting events. This is a handy list of some of those events, which includes the CNS Career Expo on the 16th (see below).

  2. CNS Career Expo

    September 16th, 1pm-6pm, Frank Erwin Center

    The semesterly Career Expo is already almost upon us! Looking for an internship? Or maybe a full-time position starting in the summer (yes, that far in advance)? Then go to the Career Expo!!! By my count (and by that I mean Adobe's), there are at least 25 companies interested in hiring physics majors, including places as diverse as: Applied Research Laboratories (ARL) here in Austin, athenahealth, Bloomberg, Intel, Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL), National Instruments, and Sandia National Laboratories. And many companies that don't have "Physics" listed as a desired major may be willing to bend the rules if you have programming experience or can convince them that you are very good at solving problems (a big part of physics).

    Information on expected attire and a list of companies (and some tips) can be found on the Career Design Center's website here. If you have questions, you can contact them (info on website), or ask a fellow physicist!

  3. Sense Corp Hiring

    September 11, 3pm-5pm, Rec Sports Center

    Dr. Franks from the Career Design Center passed on some information about a mid-sized consulting firm that is interested in hiring students with a strong background in mathematics after being extremely impressed with two math students last year. They will be at the McCombs (business school) Undergraduate Career Expo on the 11th. Attire requirements are here and list of all employers attending is here. Finally, here is a brief summary of Sense Corp:

    Sense Corp is a fast-growing team of exceptionally talented consultants. Our people are the best and brightest talent from leading firms and universities. The founder and many employees of Sense Corp are UT graduates, and we value UT's campus to provide quality consultants for our company. Our firm is committed to making a difference by working together with our clients on multi-level business challenges. Our strength comes from the people we hire; and we, therefore strongly support each individual's professional development. You will quickly learn to become a leader as you gain increasing responsibilities to help our clients in a broad spectrum of industries.

    Information about job postings for Sense Corp can be found on our website here, and you can direct any and all questions to
    ruth.franks@austin.utexas.edu.
